Quality & Safety Standards

Playground Repair operates under a comprehensive framework of Irish, EU, and international standards to ensure the utmost quality and safety of our services. Below is a summary of the key standards and certifications we uphold, and what each means for our clients:

  • NSAI and Irish Standards (I.S. EN 1176 & 1177): We follow the National Standards Authority of Ireland’s requirements for playground equipment and surfacing. .S. EN 1176I is the Irish adoption of the European Standard EN 1176, which sets out general safety requirements for playground equipment (e.g. spacing, guardrails, fall zones). I.S. EN 1177 corresponds to EN 1177, which specifies the impact attenuation requirements for playground surfacing – essentially determining the critical fall height that a surface can safely cushion . Together, these standards (introduced in Ireland in 1998 ) ensure that both the equipment and the ground surfaces in playgrounds provide adequate protection for children. Our compliance means every repair or installation we do contributes to a play environment that meets these safety benchmarks.
  • EN 1176 – Playground Equipment Safety: EN 1176 is the European Norm that covers a range of safety criteria for playground equipment design, installation, and maintenance. While Playground Repair focuses on surfacing, we are deeply familiar with EN 1176 requirements to ensure that our work on the ground interfaces safely with playground structures. For example, we maintain appropriate fall zones and surfacing depths under swings, slides, and climbing units as specified by EN 1176 to prevent injuries. We support clients in conducting the annual independent inspections recommended by EN 1176 , and we ensure any surface-related issues that could cause non-compliance (like insufficient surface depth in high fall areas) are promptly corrected. Adhering to EN 1176 is fundamental to providing a safe play space, and our team’s expertise in this standard makes us an invaluable partner for playground owners looking to meet their legal and safety obligations.
  • EN 1177 – Impact Attenuating Surfacing: EN 1177 is the European standard that focuses specifically on playground surfacing’s ability to absorb impact. It defines the test method for determining the Critical Fall Height – the maximum drop height from which a surface will adequately cushion a fall . We only use surfacing systems that have been laboratory tested and certified to EN 1177’s requirements for impact attenuation. This means that when we install or repair a wetpour surface, it has been proven to reduce injury risk for falls up to the heights claimed. Our team is knowledgeable in EN 1177 testing procedures and results, ensuring that the thickness of rubber we lay down corresponds to the needed fall height protection (for example, areas under a 2m high platform will have surfacing tested to protect a 2m fall). By complying with EN 1177, we provide assurance that your playground’s surfacing offers the shock absorption necessary to significantly reduce head injury criteria in the event of a fall.
  • BS 7188 – Performance Testing for Surfacing: BS 7188 is a British Standard widely used alongside EN 1177 to ensure surfacing materials perform well in real-world conditions. While EN 1177 focuses on impact attenuation, BS 7188 provides additional test methods and performance limits for factors like abrasive wear, slip resistance, indentation, and flammability . Playground Repair conducts or relies on BS 7188 tests for the wetpour materials we use, meaning our surfaces are verified to resist wear-and-tear from foot traffic and weather, remain slip-resistant even when wet, and have low ignition risk (important for vandal-resistance). By adhering to BS 7188 criteria, we ensure that our installed surfaces maintain their safety characteristics (proper friction, no hazardous wear) over time, not just on day one. In practical terms, this standard guarantees a longer-lasting, safer surface for the community.
  • I.S. EN 15330-1 – Sports and Play Area Surfacing: For clients incorporating multi-use games areas (MUGAs), outdoor gyms, or synthetic turf sections into their play areas, we comply with .S. EN 15330-1I. This is the Irish (and European) standard “Surfaces for sports areas – Synthetic turf and needle-punched surfaces primarily designed for outdoor use – Part 1: Specification” . It ensures that artificial grass or similar surfacing used in playgrounds and sports zones meets specific criteria for durability, shock absorption, and player safety. Our team uses EN 15330-1 as a guideline when advising on or installing turf zones (like artificial grass safety mats or combining wetpour with turf for aesthetics), ensuring those materials are of high quality and suitable for intense use. By following this standard, we extend our safety commitment beyond traditional wetpour into all types of soft surfacing a playground might feature.
  • Health & Safety Authority (HSA) Guidelines: Playground Repair operates under the regulations of Ireland’s Health and Safety Authority for construction and maintenance activities. We implement rigorous safety management on every site – including risk assessments, method statements, and trained personnel – in line with the Safety, Health and Welfare at Work Act and relevant HSA codes of practice. Our staff all hold the SOLAS Safe Pass certification (mandatory for working on construction sites in Ireland) and any necessary CSCS qualifications for equipment operation, ensuring a safety-first approach in our projects. We also have designated PSDP/PSCS (Project Supervisors for Design and Construction Stage) as required for larger projects, taking responsibility for co-ordinating safety from project planning through execution. Clients can be confident that our worksites are managed with top-notch safety diligence, protecting everyone from our crew to the public.
  • Quality & Environmental Standards: In addition to playground-specific standards, we voluntarily adhere to international quality benchmarks to deliver the best service. Our ISO 9001:2015 certification means we maintain a rigorous Quality Management System – every process from design to installation and aftercare is documented and continually improved for efficiency and reliability. For environmental stewardship, our ISO 14001:2015 certification ensures that we minimize waste, recycle materials (our rubber granules are sourced and disposed of responsibly), and prevent pollution during our projects. Furthermore, we are certified to ISO 45001:2018 (Occupational Health & Safety Management), reflecting our robust internal safety protocols that go above and beyond basic compliance. These certifications are audited by independent bodies (including NSAI), giving clients added assurance of our professionalism. In short, Playground Repair runs on systems that prioritize quality outcomes, worker safety, and environmental care at every step.
  • Industry Accreditations: As a mark of our credibility and commitment to best practices, Playground Repair has obtained several esteemed industry accreditations. We are pre-qualified as a Constructionline Gold member, which vets our company on criteria like technical ability, financial stability, and ethical conduct. We have achieved accreditation from CHAS (Contractors Health and Safety Assessment Scheme), meaning our health & safety practices have been independently verified to meet high standards – a credential valued by many clients in construction and public sectors. Similarly, we are an Alcumus SafeContractor approved contractor, underlining that our operations meet UK and Ireland requirements for risk management . In Ireland, we are one of the few playground surfacing specialists to earn the Safe-T-Cert certification , a third-party certification of our safety management system recognized across the construction industry. Additionally, we engage with professional bodies: for instance, we collaborate with the Association of Play Industries (API) in the UK and support local initiatives to improve play standards. Our inspectors and technicians undergo training and certification by the Register of Play Inspectors International (RPII), ensuring they stay current with the latest in play safety inspection protocols. All of these accreditations and memberships exemplify our dedication to being not just compliant, but truly best-in-class in the playground repair field.
